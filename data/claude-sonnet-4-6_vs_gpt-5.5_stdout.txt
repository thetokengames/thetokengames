The `check(m)` function verifies that `m` has a binary representation of the form `1ᵏ 0^(k−1)` — that is, exactly *k* leading ones followed by exactly *k−1* trailing zeros, with nothing else. This means `m = 2^(k−1) · (2^k − 1)`.

Both the 8-digit number `n` itself, and the sum of its decimal digits, must satisfy this binary pattern.

For an 8-digit number to satisfy `check`, we need `k = 13`: this gives `2^12 · (2^13 − 1) = 4096 · 8191 = 33550336` (the only 8-digit value with this form). Its digit sum is `3+3+5+5+0+3+3+6 = 28 = 11100₂`, which is `1³ 0²` — satisfying `check(28)` as well (k=3, 2 trailing zeros = 3−1 ✓).

The binary structure `1ᵏ 0^(k−1)` is precisely the form of even perfect numbers (Euler's theorem), making this an oblique double perfect-number check: `n` itself must be an even perfect number, and so must its digit sum.
