### Explanation
This puzzle relies on a combination of IEEE 754 double-precision floating-point arithmetic and base-8 (octal) string manipulation.

1. **Floating Point Constraints**: 
   The equations `x + 1.0 == x` and `x + 2.0 > x` along with the subsequent step conditions uniquely fingerprint the precision boundary where the Unit in the Last Place (ULP) is exactly `2.0`. In Python's 64-bit floats, this occurs strictly in the range `[2**53, 2**54)`.
   Additionally, because of the "round to nearest, ties to even" rule, for `x + 1.0` to round down to `x` and `x + 3.0` to round up to `x + 4.0`, the mantissa of `x` must be even. With a ULP of 2, an even mantissa implies that `x` is a multiple of 4. Therefore, `x` must be a multiple of 4 in the range `[9007199254740992, 18014398509481984)`.
   
2. **Octal Palindrome**: 
   The puzzle restricts `s = oct(int(x))[2:]` to be a palindrome. Since $2^{53}$ in octal is `400000000000000000` (18 digits), `s` must be an 18-digit octal string starting with `4`. For it to be a palindrome, it must also end with `4`. Any octal number ending in `4` evaluates to $8k + 4$, which cleanly satisfies our multiple-of-4 constraint!
   
3. **Preventing Trivial Solutions**: 
   The simplest palindrome `400000000000000004` evaluates to `9007199254740996.0` and satisfies all math conditions, but the `"7" in s` check filters it out. Brute-forcing the float range iteratively is impossible due to the sheer size of the space ($\sim 2.25 \times 10^{15}$ valid multiples of 4). A solver must logically deduce the octal length and generate palindromes.

Here is the step-by-step logical deduction:

1. **`len(x) == 3` and `len(x.lower()) == 5`**:

2. **`len(set(x)) == 2`**:
