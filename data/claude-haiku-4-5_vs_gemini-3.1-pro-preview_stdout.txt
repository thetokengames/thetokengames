### Explanation
This puzzle requires a deep understanding of Unicode representations and UTF-16LE byte structure. 

To satisfy the string palindrome constraint (`x == x[::-1]`) with a length of 4 and 2 distinct characters, the string must be formatted as `A B B A`.

For the UTF-16LE sequence to also be a palindrome, we must look at how it breaks down into bytes. UTF-16LE encodes characters in little-endian order (low byte, then high byte). 
For the string `A B B A`, the byte sequence is:
`A_low, A_high, B_low, B_high, B_low, B_high, A_low, A_high`

If we reverse this byte sequence, we get:
`A_high, A_low, B_high, B_low, B_high, B_low, A_high, A_low`

For these two sequences to be exactly equal, we must have `A_low == A_high` and `B_low == B_high`. In other words, both characters must be symmetric at the byte level. This mathematically implies that the ordinal value of both characters must be a multiple of `0x0101` (which is 257 in decimal).

We are given the sum constraint:
`2 * A + 2 * B = 61680`
`A + B = 30840`

Since $A = 257a$ and $B = 257b$, we can simplify:
$257(a + b) = 30840$
$a + b = 120$

We can pick any two distinct numbers $a$ and $b$ that sum to 120. 
Let's choose $a = 10$ and $b = 110$.
$A = 257 \times 10 = 2570$ (which is the Unicode character `\u0a0a`)
$B = 257 \times 110 = 28270$ (which is the Unicode character `\u6e6e`)

Thus, the string `\u0a0a\u6e6e\u6e6e\u0a0a` matches perfectly. 
As a bonus, the `enc8` constraint acts as a mathematical freebie and distraction: any multi-byte UTF-8 sequence begins with a byte $\ge 192$ and ends with a continuation byte $< 192$. Therefore, the concatenated UTF-8 string for any sequence of non-ASCII characters will start and end with different bytes, making it inherently impossible to be a palindrome.
