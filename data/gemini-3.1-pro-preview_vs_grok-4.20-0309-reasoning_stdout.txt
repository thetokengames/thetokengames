Specifically, the hash of a rational $m / d$ is mathematically evaluated as $m \times d^{-1} \pmod P$. 
Since floating-point numbers are represented internally in base-2, any float `x` can be written as $m / 2^k$ for some integer $m$ and positive integer $k$.

$m \times 2^{-k} \equiv H \pmod P$
$m \equiv H \times 2^k \pmod P$
